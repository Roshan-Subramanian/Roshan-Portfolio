Recent Work

Experimental Rig for Laser-Induced Cavitation Bubble Generation

Designed and constructed a complex experimental rig for laser-induced cavitation bubble generation. Absolute structural alignment—achieved via specialized level gauges and exact laser focusing optics—was crucial to accurately capture extreme, high-speed multiphase physics. Engineered a synchronized Schlieren optical setup to visualize and isolate microsecond-level shock wave emission and transient fluid boundaries.

Resolving a 100-Year-Old Fluid Mechanics Debate

Engineered a synchronized >100,000 fps multi-modal optical pipeline to definitively prove that shock waves—not microjets—dominate peak wall loading during cavitation. (Featured Article, Physics of Fluids).

Sub-15μs Micro Cold-Wire Sensor Fabrication

Designed and deployed custom micro-sensors in a cleanroom environment, bypassing commercial thermal-fluid measurement limits.

HIFU Biomedical Tumor Ablation Support

Mapped critical acoustic thresholds for cavitation clouds to directly accelerate the hardware development of non-invasive biomedical devices.

High-Speed Data Acquisition Pipelines

Developed automated Python and MATLAB architectures for high-throughput, microsecond-resolution signal processing.

Computational & Theoretical Modeling

Derived analytical models to validate empirical data against complex Navier-Stokes and potential flow assumptions.
